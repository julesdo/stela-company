    ### Synopsis

    Inspired by the ancient myth of Médée and Jason, this creation explores the
    central themes: love, trust, betrayal, rejection, fury, cruelty. The
    passage from absolute gift to violent rejection. A crossing where language,
    gesture and breath intertwine to translate these tensions through body and
    music.

    No imposed style: a free reading, freed from codes and eras. Gesture as a
    universal language. Each fragment is born from doubt, bodily intensity and
    an inner flame that never goes out.

    The musical writing, composed by Matthieu Lecoq who improvises and plays
    live, creates a living material made of rhythms, voices, noises, silences.
    Music does not support: it dialogues, interrupts, disappears to better make
    bodies heard. Between improvisation and composition, it embraces the moving
    dramaturgy of Médée.
